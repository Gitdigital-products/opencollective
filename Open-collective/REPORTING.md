# GitDigital Financial Reporting

**Last Updated: March 15, 2025**

## Overview

This document outlines our financial reporting framework, including reporting schedule, content requirements, and archive of past reports.

## Reporting Philosophy

GitDigital is committed to **radical transparency** in all financial matters. Our reports are designed to be:

- **Accessible** – Understandable by non-financial audiences
- **Comprehensive** – Cover all material financial activity
- **Timely** – Published on predictable schedule
- **Verifiable** – Traceable to source transactions
- **Actionable** – Inform community decision-making

## Report Types

### 1. Monthly Financial Snapshots

Published within 10 business days of month-end.

**Contents:**
- Executive summary
- Income for the month
- Expenses for the month (categorized)
- Top 5 transactions
- Balance sheet
- Budget vs. actual variance
- Notable events or changes
- Upcoming large expenses

**Audience:** All community members

### 2. Quarterly Reports

Published within 15 business days of quarter-end.

**Contents:**
- All monthly snapshot contents
- Quarter-over-quarter trends
- Grant progress updates
- Milestone completion status
- Detailed variance analysis
- Forecast for next quarter
- Risk assessment update
- Governance decisions made

**Audience:** All stakeholders, including sponsors

### 3. Annual Reports

Published within 30 days of fiscal year-end.

**Contents:**
- Year in review narrative
- Complete financial statements
- Audit results (if applicable)
- Grant impact summaries
- Contributor statistics
- Governance effectiveness review
- Strategic outlook
- Transparency metrics
- Acknowledgments

**Audience:** Public, press, potential sponsors

### 4. Special Reports

Published as needed for specific purposes.

**Examples:**
- Grant completion reports
- Event financial summaries
- Audit findings
- Governance proposals

## Data Sources

All reports are generated from:

1. **Open Collective API** – Transaction data
2. **GitHub** – Contribution data, grant milestones
3. **Manual entries** – Off-chain transactions (rare)
4. **Bank statements** – For fiat accounts

## Verification

Reports can be verified by:

- Cross-referencing with Open Collective public ledger
- Comparing to GitHub activity
- Reviewing linked expense documentation
- Requesting additional details from Finance Committee

## Metrics and KPIs

We track the following key metrics:

### Financial Health
- **Runway**: Months of operating expenses covered
- **Income diversity**: % from top source
- **Expense efficiency**: % to direct development
- **Reserve ratio**: % of annual budget in reserve

### Grant Effectiveness
- **Completion rate**: % of grants completed
- **Time to completion**: Average months
- **Cost per grant**: Average amount
- **Impact score**: Community-rated (1-5)

### Community Engagement
- **Contributors**: Number of unique expense submitters
- **Recipients**: Number of grant recipients
- **Reviewers**: Number of expense reviewers
- **Geography**: Countries represented
